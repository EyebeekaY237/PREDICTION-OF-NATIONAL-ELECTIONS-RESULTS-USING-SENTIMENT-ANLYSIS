Presidential Election Timeline

1999 Election

Date: February 27, 1999

Transition: Return to civilian rule after military regime

Winner: Olusegun Obasanjo (PDP) with 62.8% of votes

Significance: Marked the beginning of the Fourth Republic

2003 Election

Date: April 19, 2003

Winner: Olusegun Obasanjo (PDP) re-elected with 61.9%

Controversy: Widespread allegations of electoral fraud

Voter Turnout: 69.1%

2007 Election

Date: April 21, 2007

Winner: Umaru Yar'Adua (PDP) with 70% of votes

Observation: Considered one of the most flawed elections

Outcome: Yar'Adua acknowledged flaws and promised reforms

2011 Election

Date: April 16, 2011

Winner: Goodluck Jonathan (PDP) with 58.9% of votes

Improvement: Considered more credible than previous elections

Aftermath: Post-election violence in northern states

2015 Election

Date: March 28, 2015

Winner: Muhammadu Buhari (APC) with 53.9% of votes

Historic: First opposition victory in Nigerian history

Peaceful Transition: Incumbent conceded defeat

2019 Election

Date: February 23, 2019

Winner: Muhammadu Buhari (APC) re-elected with 55.6%

Controversy: Election postponed one week before voting

Technology: First use of card readers nationwide

2023 Election

Date: February 25, 2023

Winner: Bola Tinubu (APC) with 36.6% of votes

Innovation: BVAS technology introduced

Contestation: Results challenged in court by opposition

Evolution of Nigeria's Electoral Process

From Paper Ballots to Digital Systems

Nigeria's electoral process has undergone significant changes since independence:

  • 1959-1998: Manual voting with paper ballots
  • 1999-2010: Introduction of standardized ballot papers
  • 2011: First use of temporary voter cards
  • 2015: Introduction of Permanent Voter Cards (PVCs)
  • 2019: First nationwide use of card readers
  • 2023: Implementation of Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS)
